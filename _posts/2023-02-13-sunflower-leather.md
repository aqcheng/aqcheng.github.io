there was a lost child at the county fair, legs dangling off of a huge, white-painted lawnchair the size of a small car. you were beside her as she spun the clouds floating in the blue june sky into her cotton candy dreams. she had a smile as wide as a bicycle wheel.

i’d like to say that she was a sunflower in a bright summer dress. but we’re not like that, are we? we paint ourselves black because we fear we will melt like candles in the gentle sun. we bury our cores. we bleed and hide the stains in darkness. so in inked wings and red hair and a heavy leather jacket, i mourned the death of the person i was. 

but that day, at the top of a giant wheel, the world was beneath our hearts, and i bared my soul to the setting sun. you loved me, in that brief eternity. you held me like the sky. if only we could fly into the sun together instead of saying goodbye–i a white dress ablaze and you an ocean of kind tears;

but i stayed planted to the earth. i bloom because i am still alive. i am a sunflower in a leather jacket. i am twenty years old.
